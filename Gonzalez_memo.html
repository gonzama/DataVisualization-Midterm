We have found some findings to help increase revenue for our Regular flavor cereal. In addition, we have discovered the potential of Cinnamon Toast to become an outstanding product especially during the end of year sales.

With this, we investigated more on whether having ads plays a part of the units sold. Base on the data set, we know that GM ran a total of 7189 ads throughout the year while Kelloggs ran almost double with a total of 12183 ads. We also noticed that there is a relationship between having ads and units sold:

We can see there is a slight drop in Q4 in cereal sales and a bigger drop in advertisements for all companies. Could it be that cereal is less desired during the holidays? Or ads are more expensive to run during those 3 months?

Since Kelloggs takes most of the pie for Regular like we previously discovered, we dug deeper into their pricing with and without promotion and compared it with our company’s pricing:

GM’s median price per volume for flavor Regular is significantly higher than Kelloggs’. We currently stand at $5 per volume while Kelloggs has theirs at $3 per volume. Even with promotions, our price is still higher than Kelloggs’s normal price. That explains the higher units sold on Regular flavor for Kelloggs than GM.

Based on our findings, Kelloggs seem to get more market share due to the higher amount of advertisements and promotion in addition to the lower price. We suggest researching more strategically to drop the price per volume for Regular flavored cereal so that we can gain more sales on that. This limited-time flavor will attract customers to purchase and increase the end-of year sales as we have seen affects all companies. In addition, for Q3 and 4, we should offer more promotions for Cinnamon Toast since it’s our signature cereal and strongly stands against the cold weather.
